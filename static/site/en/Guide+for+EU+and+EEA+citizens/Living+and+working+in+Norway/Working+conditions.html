Working environment and safety

You are entitled to a safe workplace that complies with Norwegian law when you work in Norway. Read more about occupational safety and health.

Remember to get a written employment contract

When you start working you are entitled to receive a written employment contract from your employer. Read more about what an employment contract shall contain. 

You are entitled to get salary, holiday and time off

Everyone who works in Norway is entitled to get pay, regulated working hours and holiday.

  • Pay and minimum rates of pay
  • Working hours and overtime
  • Holiday and holiday pay

HSE card for the cleaning and contruction industry

If you work in the cleaning or construction industry you need an HSE card issued by the Norwegian Labour Inspection Authority. Your employer is responsible for applying for this. Self-employed people must apply themselves.

  • Read more about HSE cards in the building and construction industry 
  • Read more about HSE cards for cleaning services

Machine operator licence

In order to use your foreign Machine operator licence in Norway, you need permission from the Norwegian Labour Inspection Authority.

Getting help from trade unions

If you have any questions about working conditions or pay you can get help from a trade union.  Everyone who works in Norway, regardless of nationality, can join a trade union.
